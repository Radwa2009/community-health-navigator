        Prototype scope

        This is a frontend demo using fictional, de-identified data. A production version would require an approved backend, authentication, role-based access, audit logs, organization-approved privacy policies, and staff training.

        Pilot plan & success metrics

        Two-week pilot with one team

        1. Observe first (days 1–3): shadow the current workflow — intake, follow-up, and reporting — before changing anything.
        2. Train 2–3 staff members (days 4–5): hands-on sessions using this demo and the in-app staff guide.
        3. Run in parallel (week 2): the team uses the tool alongside their existing process for new intakes and follow-ups.
        4. Collect staff feedback: short daily check-ins plus an end-of-pilot usability conversation.
        5. Revise based on real staff behavior: change the tool to fit how people actually work, not the other way around.

        What success looks like

        • Fewer overdue follow-ups — measured before and after the pilot.
        • Less time to produce the weekly report — compared with the team's current method.
        • More complete intake notes — fewer cases flagged with missing consent, contact method, or follow-up dates.
        • Staff usability feedback — would the team keep using it after the pilot, and what would they change first?

        Demo walkthrough (about 2 minutes)

        1. Start on the Dashboard — notice the "Needs human review" panel that surfaces high-urgency and incomplete cases first.
        2. Open a high-priority case — select a family in the review list to slide open the case detail panel.
        3. Review the missing information checklist — see how the tool flags gaps like unconfirmed consent or needs without referrals, alongside suggested next steps labeled for staff review.
        4. Copy an AI prompt — go to the AI assistant, pick a case, and copy the "Summarize intake notes" template to see how staff would use Claude with de-identified data.
        5. Generate the weekly report — one click builds a funder-ready summary with a copy button, including cases needing review and suggested operational improvements.
